Ras Mohammed National Park

Ras Muhammad National Park is one of the stunning Egypt national parks found at the marvelous southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, offering stunning views of the Gulf of Suez to the west and the Gulf of Aqaba to the east plus the celestial skies of Egypt that contain the most incredible star constellations. This national park was established for the protection of marine and terrestrial wildlife due to harmful fishing practices which was transformed into a magical hub for eco-tourism. The name "Ras Muhammad" refers to a headland with a cliff profile resembling a bearded man's face. The park covers 480 square kilometers, with a mix of land and water areas situated at the convergence of three tectonic plates, leading to seismic activity and underwater caves.

The park also features mangrove forests, and desert habitats, and serves as a stopover for migrating birds. Ras Muhammad experiences a dry climate with minimal winter rainfall and scorching summers. It has diverse coral reefs, including over 220 coral species, popular for diving. The park is home to more than 1,000 fish species, sea turtles, and various other marine life. On land, you can find acacia trees, dum palms, and ephemeral herbs and grasses in the area. After sunset, everyone will take a telescope to witness the allure of the night sky, the stars, and the locations of these celestial distant planets about 45 minutes you will take pictures of the night sky where you will capture the finest sensations of mythical mystery and divine glamour.


Marsa Alam

Marsa Alam is a magnificent growing tourist attraction on the marvelous Egypt's Red Sea coast which is known for its crystal-clear waters and white sandy beaches. Marsa Alam is not just about its pristine beaches and celestial skies; it's also a haven for water sports enthusiasts in Egypt. This incredible tropical resort in 1995 has become a popular destination, often referred to as the "Egyptian Maldives". It is located 274 km south of Hurghada which covers 38,433 km2 and is home to approximately 11,497 residents. The town is also a hub for kitesurfing and safaris plus a marvelous center for stargazing to witness the heavenly celestial creations of the blessed skies of Egypt.

Marsa Alam has a tropical hot desert climate with steady temperatures. The magical Red Sea's temperature ranges from 22 to 29°C throughout the year. One of the most famous beaches in Marsa Alam is the phenomenal Abu Dabab where turtles and unique marine wildlife like crocodilefish and octopuses can be observed. Some of them have the enchanting Emerald Mines and the epic Temple of Seti I at Khanais.

Wadi El Gemal Reserve has a marvelous space of around 7,450 km2 filled with diverse landscapes, from islands and coral reefs to mangroves. It is a habitat for various wildlife, including ibex, gazelles, birds, sea creatures like the dugong and whale shark, and numerous plant species. The reserve features the Temple of Sket and aims to protect the environment while promoting the local Ababda tribes' crafts and culture. This magnificent reserve has a magical entrance leading to a magical center where everyone can caster their eyes on amazing telescopes displaying epic skies, golden stars, and celestial constellations while being surrounded by colossal mountains and Bedouin villages that contain incredible one-of-a-kind culture.


El-Gouna Desert

El Gouna is a majestic tourist resort city in Egypt which was established in 1990 and situated on the Red Sea, just 20 kilometers north of Hurghada which makes it part of the Red Sea Riviera and a host city for the El Gouna Film Festival. The city boasts 10 kilometers of coastline, comprising 20 islands surrounded by lagoons, and it's conveniently located 25 kilometers from Hurghada International Airport.

The architecture in El Gouna is designed by European and American architects, reflecting traditional rural Egyptian styles, akin to those found in the Egyptian countryside and Nubian villages. It's a hub for water sports, with numerous beaches like Zeytuna Beach, located on its own island, Mangroovy Beach, Moods Beach, and those associated with various hotels. For those seeking adventure and entertainment, there is a wide range of activities to enjoy in El Gouna. The city has three main areas: Downtown, Tamr Henna Square, and the Abu Tig Marina. Additionally, it features the El Gouna Mosque and a Coptic Orthodox Church called the Church of St. Mary and the Archangels. El Gouna enjoys year-round sunny weather with temperatures ranging from 17°C to 33°C.

The average humidity is 22%, and it is classified as having a hot desert climate and a comfortable climate, especially during the nighttime when everyone will get to shed light on the magnificent planets, stars, and the Milky Way. Everyone will enjoy a thrilling super safari to the edge of the desert in order to observe the brink of the universe that holds the remote epic treasures of the Milky Way that uncover the incredible facts on how the heavens came into existence while being surrounded by the mythical tales and wonders of the Egyptian Sahara.
